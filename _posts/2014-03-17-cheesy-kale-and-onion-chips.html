Before I went raw I baked my kale chips and was totally addicted. When I went raw and was searching high and low for an alternative. Then came the kale craze in stores. Kale chips everywhere. I still couldn’t find one to satisfy my taste. The reason, I discovered, is I like TONS of topping on my kale chips. So I started making my own.

I make these with any kind of kale, dinosaur kale, green kale or purple kale. The only difference is the texture, the taste is the same. I find the purple and green kale to be fluffier than the dinosaur kale. I LOAD the creamy ‘cheesy’ topping on each and every bit of the kale. Then I discovered that I could do the same thing with onions and was I in heaven. Kale and onion chips are one of my go­-to snacks.

I like to make kale and onion chips at the same time because the recipe is the exact same. I just double the recipe. I always seem to have extra onions and kale laying around so we have these at our house all of the time.

"Cheesy" Kale Chips Recipe

Yield: 2 bunches worth – would probably make 3­-4 trays
2 bunches kale stemmed and torn into pieces 1 cup water
1 c raw sunflower seeds
1 c raw cashews
1 red bell pepper seeded
1 c nutritional yeast
juice of 1 lemon
one whole lemon peeled
pinch of cayenne
pinch of sea salt
Method:

1. Wash and stem kale
2. Tear into chip size pieces
3. In a blender, combine all ingredients except the kale. Blend well and season to taste. More water may be necessary to blend; just keep in mind that you are aiming for a thick, batter­like mixture that will coat the kale.
4. In a large bowl, thoroughly mix the dressing mixture in with the kale, using your hands to rub it in so that it is dispersed evenly over all the kale.
5. Spread the coated kale on dehydrator trays in a single layer (using ParaFlexx sheets beneath).
6. Dehydrate at 100 degrees for 12 hours or until dry and crispy.
7. Store in an airtight container.

“Cheesy” Onion Chips

Yield: 2 onions worth – would probably make 3­-4 trays
2 onions cut in rounds like onion rings 1 cup water
1 c raw sunflower seeds
1 c raw cashews
1 red bell pepper seeded
1 c nutritional yeast
juice of 1 lemon
one whole lemon peeled
pinch of cayenne
pinch of sea salt
Method:

1. Peel and cut onions in rounds like onion rings
2. In a blender, combine all ingredients except the onion. Blend well and season to taste. More water may be necessary to blend; just keep in mind that you are aiming for a thick, batter­like mixture that will coat the kale.
3. In large bowl, thoroughly mix the dressing mixture in with the onion, using your hands to rub it in so that it is dispersed evenly over all the onion.
4. Spread the coated onion on dehydrator trays in a single layer (using ParaFlexx sheets beneath).
5. Dehydrate on 105 degrees for about 12 hours, or until crisp.
7. Store in an airtight container.
