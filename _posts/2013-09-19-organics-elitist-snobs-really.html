Very weary of people suggesting that organics is for elitist snobs. There are soooo many ways to eat well on a budget!

  • 1.) Blend in stead of juice--if you have to.
  • 2.) Join a CSA or visit your local farmers markets
  • 3.) Grow your own food--sprouts & grasses are easy-peasy & require just a 10 day growing period. Trust me, if I can do this, anyone can. $1.50 per tray, vs $3.50 per baggie in supermarket
  • 4.) Eat soup! Do you have any idea how FAR soup stock goes? Make a giant batch & freeze for many, many meals.
  • 5.) Do your own jar spouting. Dried mung, lentils, garbanzo, fenugreek etc Start with just an inch of dried in a one quart Jar. 48 hours later, your jar is FULL of FRESH LIVING FOOD!
  • 6.) Healthy grains are still fairly inexpensive & go a LONG way!
THIS IS NOT ABOUT WILL POWER. IT IS ABOUT SKILL POWER!

Lisa Wilson, The Raw Food Institute
