by Lisa Wilson


I make nut mylks all of the time but also have a need for coconut mylk for raw curries, pad thai’s, etc. This inspired me to make my own coconut mylk. In doing some quick research I learned that to make a coconut mylk you must use a mature coconut which is slightly harder to open in my opinion than the young thai coconut. I must admit it took me at least 30 minutes to open and prepare properly but left me feeling very accomplished after.

Before we get started, make sure you have the right kind of coconut! A mature coconut has a hard shell and some hair on the outside. This is the kind you want to use.


A young thai coconut - shown below - has no shell and a soft outside. This is not the kind you want to use to make coconut mylk!


1. First I used my hammer to crack the coconut then I pried it open. You can also use a large knife to open the coconut. The coconut water comes squirting out as soon as you crack the coconut so be prepared. I can’t resist drinking it right away.


2. Once it was in large sections/pieces I hammered the shell more and the meat popped out out of the shell.


3.Then I peeled it with a small peeling knife until just the meat was left.


Which took forever.


The aftermath was messy!


4. I used the meat from one coconut and placed it in the vita-mix along with 3-4 cups of water. Then I blended it on high speed for 2 minutes.


5. From there I simply poured it through a nut mylk bag and separated the mylk from the pulp.


The end result is magical. The taste of handmade coconut mylk was so satisfying. Enjoy!
